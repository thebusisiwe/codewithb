Before You Code

It is possible to start coding immediately, in-fact that's where most courses or bootcamps begin. But coding is hard and if you don't prepare properly you will get overwhelmed and quit too soon. So this page is about preparation.


Get Okay With Tech

You need to make sure that you are comfortable with and relatively safe on your devices. That means taking a basic computer course. Microsoft and LinkedIn have a free course that comes with a certificate of completion.

The Language Thing

Then, there's the language thing. See you have to accept that the western world of coding is english-based. All of the courses, documentation and most tutorials will be in english. This is not to discourage you, this is so that you can prepare yourself.

You can prepare yourself by getting comfortable with reading and writing in English. Looking up coding tutorials in the language you are most fluent in will also help but improving fluency in reading and writing in English is something you have to do. Being able to code means that you can read documentation to learn anything. Most of this documentation is also heavy in jargon, so looking up the meaning of words is something you will have to become comfortable with.

Look It Up

Seriously, you have to look things up. If you are confused spend more time and do not move on until you understand. Ask the internet. Make Google and stackoverflow your friends. Depend on them by looking things up, researching things, using them as tools. Research skills are vital in your coding journey - they are important in life - but for you to become successful at learning to code and then coding professionally you must know how to research various topics.

Coding is a type of problem-solving. It is using computational technology to solve real world problems. So though you've decided to learn how to code, understand that in doing so you have also decided learn how to be a problem-solver. To solve a problem I must understand the problem and, to understand the problem I must know how to research.

It's All In Your Head

The foundational skill of anything in life is knowing exactly what it is and that it can happen - that is about your headspace. Your headspace is so important in all of this. You will get overwhelmed. You will get tired. You will want to quit. Your ability to keep going is so important for learning. There is a lot out there about growth mindset, I will not get into it. I will just recommend a YouTube playlist.

The Struggle Is Real

Programming is difficult but that is not enough of a reason to quit. It is supposed to be difficult because there will always be a new problem that you can try solving with code.So you must be reasonable about your expectations. You must be reasonable while being persistent.

Not giving up works. That's why building the right mindset is important and worthwhile. You need to keep learning and building. Yet, you will still feel like you are faking it. It is normal to feel like you are faking it. That is proof that you've been learning this whole time - it is proof that you have learnt enough to know what's good work vs what's not. So keep going. Using this improved taste-level in your learning is what will make you the programmer you want to be.

So remember this when it gets hard - it is supposed to get hard. It is hard because you are on the right track so, keep going.
